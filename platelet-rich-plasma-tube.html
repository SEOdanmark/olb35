Frequently Asked Questions

What can be 3 complications of PRP?

Complications of PRP therapy may include infection at the injection site, pain or discomfort, and bruising or swelling. In rare cases, allergic reactions or tissue damage may occur. It's essential to discuss potential risks with your healthcare provider before undergoing PRP treatment.

Is PRP painful?

Discomfort during PRP treatment is typically minimal, although some individuals may experience mild pain or soreness at the injection site. The level of discomfort can vary depending on individual pain tolerance and the area being treated. Topical anesthesia or cold packs may be used to help manage any discomfort.

Why is platelet-rich plasma so expensive?

The cost of PRP treatment can vary depending on several factors, including the expertise of the provider, the specific condition being treated, the equipment used, and geographic location. Additionally, PRP requires specialized equipment and trained personnel for preparation and administration, which can contribute to its higher cost compared to other treatments.

Is it OK to do PRP every month?

The frequency of PRP treatments should be determined by a qualified healthcare provider based on individual needs, treatment goals, and response to therapy. While some people may benefit from monthly treatments, others may require less frequent sessions. It's essential to follow the recommended treatment plan to achieve the best results while minimizing potential risks.
