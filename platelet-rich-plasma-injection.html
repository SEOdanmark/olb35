Once the blood is collected in the PRP tubes, they are placed in a centrifuge machine.

During this spinning process, the heavier red blood cells sink to the bottom of the tube, while the lighter plasma rises to the top. After centrifugation is complete, the separated layers can be easily distinguished within the PRP tube.
